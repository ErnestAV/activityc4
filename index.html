El Salvador's Top Lakes

El Salvador is located in the Central American region. It is the smallest (even smaller than the state of Massachussetts), yet one of the prettiest countries when talking about natural touristic attractions.
It is rich in natural attractions and has an awesome tropical weather all year long. Although there are many natural attractions such as volcanos, waterfalls, and especially the beach... today, we will focus on the top lakes!

Without further ado, here are the top lakes of this awesome country:

Lago de Coatepeque (Coatepeque Lake)

The most beautiful lake in El Salvador. Period.

This breath-taking lake is one of the most beautiful crater lakes ever seen! Located near one of the best national parks in the country, this site has everything you can wish in a lake.
There are restaurants along the coast, kayaking, boat trips, fishing, scuba diving, jet skis, and you can even swim there! There are some great hotels and Aibnbs available as well.

Lago de Ilopango (Ilopango Lake)

El Salvador's largest crater volcano.

This crater volcano touches three different departments (departments work almost as states) which include San Salvador, La Paz, and Cuscatlan.
You can go swimming, kayaking, boating, and even enjoy the beaches located on the banks of the lake. Diving is also available!
If you want a more extreme experience, you can dive to the active volcano crater and appreciate all the volcanic activity happening at the bottom of the lake.

Lago Suchitlan (Suchitlan Lake)

Colonial town + awesome lake? Sign me up!

A popular activity for tourists in El Salvador is to visit the colonial town of Suchitoto, which has the awesome Suchitlan Lake nearby. At the port there are many restaurants, souvenir shops, and a swimming pool.
Fishing, kayaking, and bird watching are among the most popular activities for visitors. An underrated gem for sure!
